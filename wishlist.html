Kitchen Tongs

I must have at least 5 different cooking tongs of all different lengths. I use them to cook (e.g., stir spaghetti around in a pot, flip food on the stove or grill) and serve food (e.g., pasta and salad) all the time. I’ve been known to pack a pair in my suitcase when I know I’ll be cooking on vacation.

Toaster Oven

I use my toaster oven all the time instead of my regular oven. It preheats so much faster than a regular oven, so it saves a lot of time and energy. I roast fish, chicken and vegetables in it for a quick and easy dinner, and have baked muffins and apple crisp in it, too (a 6-cup muffin pan or 8-inch square pan fits in my toaster oven).

Rice cooker

Since I have a small kitchen, one-trick-pony appliances rarely make it into my kitchen. The rice cooker has been one of those rare birds, though, that is worth every inch of valuable kitchen space. You don’t need a fancy rice cooker at all because even a $15 one will still yield perfectly cooked rice thanks to its built-in sensors. Brown rice and quinoa are two healthy options for sides, and both of these can be cooked perfectly in your rice cooker without any babysitting. I love having a make-ahead cooking day where I can prep these and tuck them in the fridge for our week.
